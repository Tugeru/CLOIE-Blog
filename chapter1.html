1.1 Background of the Study

This section summarizes the capstone context: higher education institutions need organized, evidence-based outcomes monitoring, while manual and fragmented workflows create reporting and accreditation challenges.

  • Computerization supports better collection, organization, and analysis of assessment evidence.
  • Outcome-based and accreditation-driven practices increase demand for structured documentation.
  • Assumption College of Davao needs a centralized approach to outcomes monitoring.
  • CLOIE is proposed to consolidate stakeholder feedback and produce outcome-aligned reports.
  • Industry changes reinforce the need for curriculum relevance and continuous improvement.

1.2 Objectives of the Study

The chapter defines CLOIE's general objective and specific goals for outcomes management, stakeholder evaluation, reporting, and confidentiality.

General Objective

Develop CLOIE to support systematic monitoring of Program Learning Outcomes and Graduate Outcomes through stakeholder-based feedback and structured reporting.

Specific Objectives

  • Enable encoding and management of PLOs and GOs per program.
  • Integrate required evaluation tools for students, graduates/alumni, and industry partners.
  • Generate consolidated outcome-attainment reports for evaluation and accreditation support.
  • Ensure confidential and anonymized data handling aligned with continuous improvement.

1.3 Significance of the Study

CLOIE is positioned to support multiple academic stakeholders through centralized, evidence-based program evaluation.

  • Assumption College of Davao: more organized outcomes monitoring and accreditation readiness.
  • Program Heads and Deans: clearer summaries for planning, review, and decision-making.
  • Faculty: stronger alignment between outcomes, evaluation results, and instruction.
  • Students and Graduating Students: better program quality and outcomes relevance.
  • Alumni and Industry Partners: direct feedback loop for employability and curriculum fit.
  • Future Researchers: practical reference for outcomes-assessment system development.

1.4 Scope and Delimitation

1.4.1 Scope

  • PLO and GO management
  • Stakeholder-based evaluation (students, graduating students, alumni, industry)
  • Outcome data collection and analysis
  • Automated reporting for program review and QA support
  • Continuous quality improvement support through PDCA-aligned review
  • Confidential and anonymized response handling

1.4.2 Delimitation

  • Not a grading system
  • Not a Learning Management System (LMS)
  • Not a Student Information System (SIS)
  • No individual student performance tracking
  • Not a full institutional accreditation management replacement
  • Limited to evaluation, monitoring, and reporting functions

1.5 Time and Place of the Study

The project is situated at Assumption College of Davao and follows the capstone development period from March 2026 to September 2026, covering analysis, design, development, testing, and documentation.

The conceptual framework follows an Input-Process-Output (IPO) model integrated with a PDCA feedback loop for continuous quality improvement.
