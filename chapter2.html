Chapter 2

Review of Related Literature, Studies, and Systems

Summary of the conceptual, empirical, and systems-level foundations that justify the development of CLOIE.

Home / Chapter 2

Related Literature Related Studies Related Systems Synthesis

2.1 Related Literature

The capstone literature review emphasizes evidence-based evaluation in higher education and the need for structured, continuous outcomes monitoring.

  • Outcome-Based Education: outcomes must be defined, measured, and reviewed.
  • Learning Outcomes Assessment: institutions need clear evidence workflows, not scattered files.
  • Quality Assurance: documentation and indicators must support internal and external review.
  • Continuous Quality Improvement: PDCA cycles rely on recurring evidence and interpretation.
  • Curriculum and Industry Alignment: outcomes should adapt to labor-market shifts.
  • Graduate Employability: post-graduation relevance is a key indicator of program quality.

2.2 Related Studies

Empirical studies in the chapter consistently show that manual and fragmented assessment workflows weaken decision-making, while integrated digital workflows improve interpretation and reporting.

  • Studies on outcomes assessment point to standardization and integration challenges.
  • Curriculum-alignment studies highlight the value of external and industry input.
  • Tracer and employability studies reveal relevance gaps after graduation.
  • Stakeholder-based studies show that multiple respondent groups are necessary.
  • Learning analytics studies demonstrate the value of dashboards for interpretation.
  • Digital assessment platform studies support centralized data capture and reporting.

2.3 Related Systems

Chapter 2 reviews existing systems to show what is already feasible and where a focused college-level platform like CLOIE can add value.

  • Penn State / Nuventive: outcomes documentation, mapping, and reporting in one platform.
  • Oklahoma State Platform: strong adoption through centralized dashboards and analysis.
  • UP QA Portal: Philippine example of centralized QA and multi-source feedback processes.
  • CHED Regional Monitoring: regulator-level move toward digital monitoring and analytics.
  • Graduate Tracer Systems: structured post-graduation data collection and interpretation.

2.4 Synthesis of Reviewed Literature, Studies, and Systems

The chapter synthesis concludes that CLOIE is justified because institutions need a scoped, practical platform that combines outcomes evaluation, stakeholder feedback, qualitative and quantitative evidence, and reporting for program-level decision-making.

  • The literature establishes the institutional demand for structured evidence.
  • The studies confirm limitations of manual and fragmented evaluation workflows.
  • The systems demonstrate real-world feasibility of centralized approaches.
  • Existing platforms do not fully match the targeted scope of CLOIE.
  • CLOIE is positioned as a focused, transparent, and auditable academic support system.
